Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about OmniBazaar

OmniBazaar is a decentralized peer-to-peer marketplace built on blockchain technology. It allows users to buy and sell physical goods, digital assets, NFTs, and services directly without intermediaries, using smart contract escrow for secure transactions.
To start selling: 1) Create an account or connect your wallet, 2) Complete optional KYC verification for higher limits, 3) Click "Create Listing" and fill in product details, 4) Set your price in XOM or supported cryptocurrencies, 5) Publish your listing to the blockchain.
XOM (OmniCoin) is the native cryptocurrency of the OmniBazaar platform. It's used for transactions, staking, governance voting, and earning rewards. XOM holders receive fee discounts and can participate in platform decisions.
Our smart contract escrow automatically holds payment when a buyer makes a purchase. Funds are only released to the seller when: 1) The buyer confirms receipt and satisfaction, 2) The delivery is confirmed via tracking, or 3) The escrow period expires without dispute. This protects both parties from fraud.
OmniBazaar charges a low 2.5% transaction fee on successful sales. XOM token holders receive discounts: 20% off with 1000+ XOM staked, 50% off with 10,000+ XOM staked. There are no listing fees, and buyers don't pay platform fees.
Disputes are resolved through our decentralized arbitration system. Community arbitrators review evidence from both parties and vote on the outcome. The smart contract automatically executes the decision. Arbitrators are rewarded in XOM for fair judgments.
While OmniBazaar primarily uses cryptocurrency, we're integrating fiat payment gateways in supported regions. Users can purchase XOM directly with credit cards or bank transfers through our partners.
Basic buying and selling doesn't require KYC. However, verified users enjoy higher transaction limits, access to premium features, and enhanced trust scores. KYC is handled by our decentralized oracle network to protect privacy.
